---
title: "Quickstart"
description: "Upload your first image and generate an alpha pattern in under a minute."
---

Follow these steps to go from a raw image to an exported alpha pattern.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the app">
    Navigate to the Image to Alpha Pattern app in your browser. You'll see the upload screen — a dark drop zone with "Drag your image here" in the center.

    <Tip>
      The app works entirely offline once loaded. You do not need an internet connection to process images.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload your image">
    Load your image in one of two ways:

    - **Drag and drop** your file directly onto the drop zone — it highlights in indigo when you hover over it.
    - **Click the drop zone** (or press Enter when it's focused) to open the system file browser.

    Accepted formats: **JPG**, **JPEG**, **PNG**, **WEBP**.

    <Info>
      PNG files with transparency are fully supported. Use the alpha threshold filter later to clean up semi-transparent edges.
    </Info>

    A loading spinner appears while the image is decoded. Once done, the workspace opens automatically.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review your image info">
    The left sidebar shows a thumbnail of your original image along with:

    - **Archivo** — the filename (truncated if long)
    - **Resolución** — the natural dimensions, e.g. `1200 × 800 px`

    This information helps you choose the right matrix size in the next step.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure the matrix">
    In the **Configurar Matriz** panel, set the grid dimensions:

    | Setting | Default | Range |
    |---------|---------|-------|
    | **Columns (N)** | 50 | 1–500 |
    | **Rows (M)** | auto (aspect ratio) | 1–500 |

    Enable **Keep original proportions** (checked by default) to automatically recalculate rows when you change columns, and vice versa.

    The cell size preview below the inputs shows how many image pixels map to each grid cell, e.g. `Cell size: 24.0 × 16.0 px · 2500 cells total`.

    <Tip>
      Start with 30–50 columns for a simple craft-friendly pattern. Increase to 100+ for detailed pixel art.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply color filters (optional)">
    Expand the **Filtros de Color** section to reduce palette complexity:

    - **Color similarity threshold** (0–50 %): merges colors that are close in RGB space. Set to 5–15 % to eliminate near-duplicate shades from photos.
    - **Alpha threshold** (0–100 %): ignores pixels below this opacity. Useful for PNG images with soft anti-aliased edges.

    Leave both at 0 % to start. You can regenerate after adjusting.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate the pattern">
    Click **Generate Pattern**. A spinner appears while the image is processed — pixel averaging runs in a Web Worker so the page stays responsive.

    Once complete, the right panel shows:

    - The pattern canvas with colored, labeled cells
    - The color palette legend
    - The export panel
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the pattern">
    Use the interactive canvas to inspect your pattern:

    - **Scroll** to zoom in and out toward the cursor.
    - **Click and drag** (in **🖐 Mover** mode) to pan around.
    - Click **Ajustar vista** to fit the full pattern back in view.

    Zoom in far enough and letter symbols (A, B, C…) appear on each cell — these are the labels you follow when crafting.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Edit individual cells (optional)">
    Click **✏️ Lápiz** to switch to the pencil tool. Select a color from the palette swatches that appear in the toolbar, then click or drag on cells to repaint them.

    Use **↩** and **↪** to undo and redo strokes.

    <Warning>
      Clicking **Generate Pattern** again clears all pencil edits and undo history. Make sure you're happy with your settings before doing a final generate.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export your pattern">
    The **Exportar Patrón** panel offers six formats:

    | Format | Button label | Best for |
    |--------|-------------|----------|
    | PNG image | **Exportar PNG** | Printing, sharing |
    | Excel workbook | **Exportar Excel** | Working in Excel |
    | Colored Excel | **Excel con color** | Visual reference in Excel |
    | CSV | **Exportar CSV** | Google Sheets import |
    | Google Sheets (colored) | **Google Sheets con color** | Collaborative crafting |
    | HTML with color | **HTML con color** | Offline browser reference |

    Click any button to download the file immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>
